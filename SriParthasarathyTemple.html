About

Located in the heart of Chennai, Sri Parthasarathy Temple is one of the oldest and most revered Vishnu temples in South India. Built in the 6th century by the Pallava king Narasimhavarman I, the temple has undergone renovations by the Vijayanagara kings and still stands as a majestic piece of Dravidian architecture.

The temple is unique for enshrining five forms of Vishnu: Yoga Narasimha, Rama, Gajendra Varadaraja, Ranganatha, and Krishna as Parthasarathy, making it a special pilgrimage site. With its intricate carvings, towering gopurams, and a sacred pond, this temple is a must-visit heritage site in Chennai.
