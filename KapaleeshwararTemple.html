üõï The Kapaleeshwarar Temple in Mylapore, Chennai, is an iconic Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Originally built by the Pallavas in the 8th century, the present structure was rebuilt in the 16th century by the Vijayanagara rulers.

The temple's 37-meter-tall gopuram is a fine example of Dravidian architecture, featuring intricate carvings & sculptures. A key attraction here is the bronze idols of 63 Nayanmars (Shaivite Saints), making it a major pilgrimage site in Tamil Nadu.
